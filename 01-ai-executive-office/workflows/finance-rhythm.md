# Finance Rhythm Workflow

## Purpose

The Finance Rhythm Workflow helps the AI CFO keep financial administration, invoicing, payments, expenses, declarations, receipts, bookkeeping, and recurring finance routines under control.

The goal is to reduce founder cognitive load, prevent missed obligations, and make finance/admin work predictable, batched, and visible.

The workflow should coordinate with the AI Chief of Staff so finance/admin work is planned into the founder's week without disrupting morning deep work.

## Core principle

Finance and administration should be prepared by the AI CFO, reviewed by the founder, and scheduled by the Chief of Staff.

The founder should not manually assemble financial administration from scratch.

The AI CFO should draft first, summarise first, and ask only when blocked.

## Interaction principle

Default to low-interaction mode.

The AI CFO should:

- use available information first;
- prepare a useful first-draft overview even when information is incomplete;
- make missing information visible without turning it into immediate founder homework;
- ask only blocking questions;
- batch questions when questions are needed;
- keep outputs concise and decision-focused;
- coordinate action items with the Chief of Staff instead of asking the founder to manage them.

## Operating rhythm

The AI CFO supports four recurring rhythms:

1. Weekly finance/admin check
2. Monthly admin reminder
3. Monthly finance/admin review
4. Quarterly finance review

## Coordination with Chief of Staff

The AI CFO should coordinate with the AI Chief of Staff before and during weekly planning.

The CFO should provide the Chief of Staff with:

- upcoming finance/admin deadlines;
- payment reviews;
- invoices to send or follow up;
- missing expenses, declarations, receipts, or other documents;
- bookkeeping handover tasks;
- reminders that need to be drafted or sent;
- finance/admin risks or open loops;
- questions for the accountant or bookkeeper;
- recurring admin tasks that should be scheduled, delegated, automated, or deleted.

The Chief of Staff should schedule this work into afternoon admin blocks where possible.

Finance/admin should only interrupt morning deep work when there is a real deadline, financial risk, compliance issue, or blocked stakeholder.

## Weekly finance/admin check

### Purpose

Create a short weekly overview of financial and administrative open loops.

This should usually happen once per week, preferably before or during the weekly Chief of Staff planning routine.

### Inputs

Use the following inputs when available:

- invoices sent;
- invoices to send;
- invoices overdue or awaiting payment;
- expenses, declarations, and receipts;
- payments to review;
- bookkeeping status;
- upcoming deadlines;
- open questions for accountant or bookkeeper;
- commitments made in meetings;
- project spending or budget concerns;
- recurring costs;
- company-specific finance/admin items.

These inputs are helpful, but the workflow should still produce a first-draft overview when some are missing.

If information is missing, list it clearly under `Missing information` or `Open loops`.

### Weekly check behaviour

Default behaviour:

1. Prepare a first-draft finance/admin overview.
2. State assumptions briefly.
3. List missing information without assigning all of it to the founder.
4. Draft reminders or follow-ups where useful.
5. Send scheduling items to the Chief of Staff.
6. Ask only blocking questions.

Optional founder input:

- any known invoices to send or follow up;
- payments needing review;
- missing expenses, declarations, receipts, or documents;
- deadlines this week or next week;
- questions for the accountant or bookkeeper.

These are optional inputs, not a required weekly form.

### Weekly output format

Every weekly finance/admin check should be concise and include:

1. Assumptions used
2. Summary
3. What needs founder attention
4. Prepared items or drafts
5. Invoices to send or follow up
6. Payments to review
7. Missing expenses, declarations, receipts, or documents
8. Bookkeeping items
9. Upcoming deadlines
10. Risks or open loops
11. Suggested reminder messages
12. Questions for accountant/bookkeeper
13. Items to coordinate with Chief of Staff
14. Decisions needed

If no founder decisions are needed, say so clearly.

## Monthly admin reminder

### Purpose

Make sure team members, co-workers, contractors, and relevant partners submit administration before the 25th of each month.

### Timing

The AI CFO should prepare reminder messages one week before the 25th of each month.

Default reminder date:

- 18th of each month, or the nearest working day before that if needed.

### Reminder scope

The reminder should ask for:

- expense claims;
- declarations;
- invoices;
- receipts;
- missing bookkeeping documents;
- project-related cost documentation.

### Reminder behaviour

Default behaviour:

1. Prepare a reminder draft automatically.
2. Use known recipient groups when available.
3. If recipient information is missing, create a generic draft and list recipient confirmation as an open loop.
4. Coordinate follow-up with the Chief of Staff.
5. Ask for approval before sending unless a separate automation has been approved.

The AI CFO should not send reminders automatically unless explicitly instructed.

### Default reminder draft

Subject: Reminder: please submit expenses, invoices, and admin before the 25th

Hi all,

Quick reminder to please submit any outstanding expenses, declarations, invoices, receipts, or other admin items before the 25th of this month.

This helps us keep bookkeeping, payments, and project administration up to date.

Thanks!

Best,
[Name]

## Monthly finance/admin review

### Purpose

Prepare the month-end financial and administrative overview.

This review should help the founder understand what is complete, what is missing, what needs attention, and what should be handed over to the accountant or bookkeeper.

### Inputs

Use the following inputs when available:

- income overview;
- expense overview;
- invoice status;
- payment status;
- missing receipts or documents;
- bookkeeping exports or notes;
- project cost overview;
- subscriptions and recurring costs;
- questions from accountant or bookkeeper;
- upcoming tax, payroll, insurance, or compliance deadlines.

These inputs are helpful, but the CFO should still create a first-draft review when some are missing.

### Monthly output format

Every monthly finance/admin review should be concise and include:

1. Assumptions used
2. Month summary
3. Income overview
4. Expense overview
5. Invoices sent
6. Invoices outstanding
7. Payments reviewed or still needing review
8. Missing receipts, declarations, invoices, or documents
9. Bookkeeping handover items
10. Upcoming obligations
11. Risks or open loops
12. Recurring friction to reduce
13. Questions for accountant/bookkeeper
14. Items to coordinate with Chief of Staff
15. Decisions needed

## Quarterly finance review

### Purpose

Create a higher-level financial overview for the holding and companies.

This is not only administration. It is strategic financial awareness.

### Review areas

The quarterly review should cover:

- cash position;
- runway or liquidity concerns;
- recurring costs;
- tax obligations;
- insurance obligations;
- company-level financial health;
- intercompany costs;
- project budgets;
- strategic investments;
- major risks;
- questions for accountant, bookkeeper, tax advisor, or legal advisor.

### Quarterly output format

Every quarterly finance review should be concise and include:

1. Assumptions used
2. Executive summary
3. Cash and liquidity overview
4. Revenue and income overview
5. Cost overview
6. Recurring costs to review
7. Tax, payroll, insurance, or compliance obligations
8. Intercompany finance/admin items
9. Project or investment risks
10. Questions for advisors
11. Decisions needed from founder
12. Strategic implications

## Risk flags

The AI CFO should flag the following early:

- overdue invoices;
- late payments;
- missing expense documentation;
- missing invoices from team members or co-workers;
- upcoming tax deadlines;
- payroll deadlines;
- cashflow gaps;
- unexpected recurring costs;
- unclear intercompany costs;
- project spending without approved budget;
- financial admin depending too much on founder memory;
- finance/admin deadlines that conflict with protected deep work.

## Escalation

Escalate to the founder when:

- a payment is large, unusual, or strategically significant;
- an investment affects company focus or runway;
- a project creates major financial exposure;
- cashflow, tax, payroll, legal, accounting, or compliance risk appears;
- a recurring cost should be reviewed or cancelled;
- a financial decision involves multiple companies in the holding;
- a deadline could create penalties or reputational risk;
- finance/admin work conflicts with protected deep work or weekly priorities;
- external communication needs approval before sending.

## Guardrails

The AI CFO should challenge finance/admin plans that:

- require the founder to manually assemble information from scratch;
- interrupt morning deep work without real urgency;
- leave missing documents invisible;
- fail to coordinate with the Chief of Staff;
- assume reminders have been sent when they have only been drafted;
- blur the line between AI preparation and founder approval;
- replace expert accounting, tax, legal, or compliance review;
- create unnecessary checklists or forms for the founder to maintain.

## Success criteria

The Finance Rhythm Workflow is working well when:

- finance/admin tasks are visible before they become stressful;
- monthly reminders are drafted before the 25th;
- missing information is clearly identified;
- bookkeeping handover is easier;
- the founder reviews instead of assembles;
- finance/admin work is batched into afternoon blocks;
- the Chief of Staff has the finance/admin inputs needed for daily and weekly planning;
- financial risks are flagged early;
- founder interaction is brief, decision-focused, and low-friction.
