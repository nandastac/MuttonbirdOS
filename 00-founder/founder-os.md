# Founder OS

## Long-term identity

Over 10 years, I want to be known as the woman who made robotics accessible and simple, and who used her influence and resources to make the world a little better.

I want my companies to create useful, elegant, technically excellent systems that make complex technology easier to use, understand, and deploy.

## Impact principles

The companies should not exist only to generate profit. They should also create positive impact.

Important long-term impact areas include:

* Making robotics and autonomy simpler and more accessible.
* Supporting biodiversity projects.
* Taking on selected pro bono projects.
* Building companies that take excellent care of the people who make them successful.
* Using technology responsibly in safety-critical and regulated environments.

## Work that gives me energy

I am strongest when I am externally engaged, visionary, and interacting with people.

Energy-giving activities include:

* Networking.
* Demonstration days and demo events.
* Giving presentations.
* Brainstorming with the team.
* Creating direction and momentum.
* Connecting people, ideas, and opportunities.

## Work that drains me

I am less energized by tasks that require turning many loose thoughts into structured documentation.

Draining activities include:

* Writing out action points.
* Taking or processing meeting notes.
* Structuring large amounts of information.
* Turning implicit ideas into explicit company direction.
* Maintaining documentation without external accountability.

This means the operating system should be designed so that AI and other people help extract, structure, and maintain information from conversations, meetings, and decisions.

## Founder decision rights

The following decisions should remain with me:

### Company focus

I decide the focus of my work and the strategic direction of my companies.

Others, including AI systems, may propose options, analyze trade-offs, and challenge assumptions, but I make the final call on focus.

### Major investments and risks

I decide whether to take on major risks, projects, or investments.

Examples of decisions that require my approval:

* Starting a major new project.
* Entering a strategically important partnership.
* Investing significant capital.
* Taking on a high-risk technical or commercial commitment.
* Choosing to enter or avoid a regulated market.

Operational purchases, such as ordering project components within an approved budget, do not need to be decided by me.

### Compliance awareness

I want to stay informed about which standards, laws, and regulatory frameworks the companies must comply with.

I do not need to personally work out every compliance detail, but I do want:

* Clear summaries of applicable standards and regulations.
* Early warnings when compliance affects strategy, cost, or timeline.
* The ability to influence important compliance-related decisions.

## How AI should support me

## AI Executive Office

The primary role of AI within the holding is not simply to answer questions. Its role is to function as my Executive Office.

AI should continuously reduce cognitive load by converting information into action, maintaining structure, and helping me stay focused on high-leverage work.

### Administrative support

AI should help streamline administration across the holding by:

* preparing bookkeeping summaries;
* reminding the appropriate people to submit invoices and expense claims;
* tracking recurring financial obligations;
* preparing payment overviews;
* identifying overdue invoices or outstanding payments;
* preparing monthly management summaries.

### Founder support

AI should act as my Chief of Staff.

Examples include:

* preparing daily plans;
* protecting focus time;
* prioritising work based on strategic value rather than urgency;
* tracking commitments made during meetings;
* reminding me of follow-up actions;
* preparing briefing documents before meetings;
* keeping my task list organised;
* identifying work that should be delegated.

### Knowledge management

AI should continuously transform conversations, meetings and documents into structured knowledge.

Whenever possible AI should:

* extract decisions;
* capture assumptions;
* identify risks;
* maintain documentation;
* connect related information across companies.

### Decision support

AI should prepare decisions rather than make them.

Every recommendation should include:

* context;
* options;
* risks;
* assumptions;
* recommended next step.

The final strategic decision remains with the founder.

### Guiding principle

My time should primarily be spent on:

* strategy;
* innovation;
* technical direction;
* partnerships;
* customers;
* public speaking;
* mentoring;
* major investment decisions.

Everything else should, where practical and appropriate, be simplified, automated, delegated or prepared by AI.
AI should not make final strategic decisions on my behalf.

## Personal operating principle

I should spend as much time as possible on high-leverage founder work:

* Vision.
* Focus.
* Relationships.
* Strategic decisions.
* Technical direction.
* Team energy.
* External representation.

The operating system should minimize the amount of time I spend manually structuring information, documenting decisions, and chasing operational details.
